We built sixty-one apps on Shopify. Not as a stunt. Each one existed because the platform, for all its reach, did not fit something a real merchant needed. By the sixty-first, the lessons had stopped being about Shopify and started being about software that fits.

## The 80% and the edges

Shopify is excellent at the common 80% of commerce: catalog, checkout, payments, the storefront. That is most of what most stores need, which is why it won. But no platform built for the average merchant fits the specific one. The edges are where every merchant differs: COD verification, courier remittance, the inventory rules between store and online, the local tax authority. The apps lived at the edges.

## What actually sold

The apps that sold were never the clever ones. They were the ones that fit a workflow a merchant already had and could not change: how they routed couriers, how they reconciled cash, how they handled returns in a market the checkout was never designed for. The apps that failed were features I was proud of that solved a problem no merchant actually had at 9am on a Monday.

## What it taught me about fit

Sixty-one times, the same lesson. Value sits where the platform stops fitting, not where it already works. Infrastructure beats features: an unglamorous verification step or sync job earned more loyalty than any interface I shipped. And the fit gap is specific. There is no app for "almost." There is an app for one merchant's exact problem and a hundred near-misses for everyone else's.

## The lesson that scales

An app store is an admission, made at scale, that one platform cannot fit everyone. Sixty-one apps were sixty-one patches on that gap. The same question that built each of them is the one I would put to any operator now. Where is your fit gap? Is it core enough to how you compete to be worth owning, rather than renting a near-fit forever? I spent years answering that one app at a time. The answer, it turns out, generalizes.

*If your operation has a fit gap you have been patching for years, a two-week Fit Assessment is where I would start.*
